Passes Paid This Season

Every 2-hour court session costs 4 open-play passes. Two people trade in 2 passes each to book it. These are the rules for who pays.

Rule 1 — Only two players on

If a day only has 2 players available, there's no decision to make — those two simply pay for the session themselves. This doesn't touch the rotation line at all; it's not their "turn," so it doesn't cost them their place.

Rule 2 — Three or four players on

With 3 or 4 players, the rotation kicks in. There's a single line of the whole roster. Whoever's at the front pays, along with the next person after them in line — but only if they're actually playing that day.

Rule 3 — Skipped players keep their spot

If the person at the front of the line isn't playing that day, they're skipped for this session — but they don't lose their place. They stay exactly where they are in line, still first up, until a day comes along where they're actually playing.

Rule 4 — Paying sends you to the back

Whoever does pay moves to the very back of the line — the longest possible wait before they're picked again. Combined with Rule 3, this guarantees no one pays twice before everyone else already ahead of them in line has had a turn.

Why it works this way

A simple "next two people" rotation breaks down as soon as someone's unavailable — if you just skip them and move on, they can end up back at the front again almost immediately, while whoever paid barely gets a break. Treating it as a real line — skipped players frozen in place, payers sent to the very back — means the wait before paying again is always as long as possible, regardless of who happens to be free on a given day.
